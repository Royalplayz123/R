1. What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by websites.

Cookies Explained

  • Definition: A cookie is a small piece of data (text file) that a website sends to your browser when you visit it.
  • Purpose: Cookies enable the website to remember your actions and preferences (such as login, region, language) over a period of time, so you don't have to re-enter them every time you return to the site.
  • Lifecycle: Cookies typically expire after a certain period unless you choose to delete them earlier.

    • 2. Essential Cookies

    Required for website functionality. Disabling these may impact your ability to use our services.

    Notice

    These cookies are necessary for the website to function. You cannot refuse them without impacting the functionality of website.

    Authentication & Security

  • Login Persistence: Keeps you logged in as you navigate between pages.
  • Cross-Site Protection: Helps prevent unauthorized access to your account.
  • Cookies: Maintains your login session securely.

    • 3. Analytical Cookies

    Help us improve user experience and analyze website traffic.

    Performance & Analytics

  • Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to analyze how visitors use our website. The data collected (IP address, browser type, landing pages) is anonymized and aggregated.
  • Page Load Time: Helps us identify slow-loading pages and optimize our content.
  • Traffic Sources: Tracks where our visitors come from (Direct, Search, Social Media) to better target our audience.
  • Heatmaps: We may use heatmaps to see which parts of our pages users click on most.

    • 4. How to Manage Cookies

    You have the option to accept or decline cookies, or to delete them.

    Browser Controls

    • Most browsers allow you to see and delete cookies that have been set.
    • You can set your browser to block all cookies or to notify you when a cookie is being set.
    • If you block all cookies, some parts of our website may not function correctly.

    Google Chrome

  • Settings: Chrome Menu > Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data > See all cookies and site data.
  • Deletion: Click on the trash icon next to the website you wish to clear.

    • Mozilla Firefox

  • Settings: Firefox Menu > Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data > Manage Data.
  • Deletion: Click "Remove Selected" or "Remove All" to clear cookies.

    • Safari (macOS/iOS)

  • Settings: Safari Menu > Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data.
  • Deletion: Select website and click "Remove". Select "Remove Now" to clear all data.
